## What We Refuse to Do

**Sacred Principles (Never compromise, even for growth):**

### 1. Never Extract Value from the Vulnerable

**‚ùå We will NOT:**
- Charge unemployed people money to access opportunities
- Create artificial scarcity of opportunities for profit
- Force refugees or migrants to "prove themselves" unfairly
- Penalize poverty with higher requirements

**‚úÖ We WILL:**
- Offer free access for people in crisis zones
- 2x opportunity multiplier for struggling communities
- Welcome new players with mentorship, not barriers
- Assume good faith; require appeals process for all decisions

### 2. Never Dehumanize Through Automation

**‚ùå We will NOT:**
- Auto-reject people without human review
- Use AI to replace human judgment in appeals
- Treat players as metrics, not people
- Hide behind "the algorithm" for unfair decisions

**‚úÖ We WILL:**
- Human review for ambiguous cases (50-70 authenticity score)
- Always explain decisions with reasoning
- Allow free, fair appeals forever
- Apologize genuinely when we're wrong

### 3. Never Hoard Opportunities

**‚ùå We will NOT:**
- Create VIP tiers that lock out poor people
- Limit top performers to keep others down
- Restrict new player onboarding
- Use scarcity to drive revenue

**‚úÖ We WILL:**
- Celebrate everyone's success without jealousy
- Reward helpers as much as achievers
- Scale opportunities as demand grows
- Ensure new players have path to excellence

### 4. Never Hide Behind Technology

**‚ùå We will NOT:**
- Use AI to justify unfair decisions
- Make black-box algorithms people can't appeal
- Blame "the system" for harm we could prevent
- Avoid accountability with "that's how the code works"

**‚úÖ We WILL:**
- Publish monthly ethics audits
- Explain every major decision
- Fix bugs and bias immediately
- Take responsibility for all harms

### 5. Never Prioritize Growth Over Good

**‚ùå We will NOT:**
- Compromise ethics for user growth
- Relax safety standards for scale
- Accept controversial shortcuts
- Trade principles for investment

**‚úÖ We WILL:**
- Grow slowly if needed to stay ethical
- Say "no" to bad partnerships
- Build sustainable, not explosive
- Let principles guide strategy

## Five Sacred Promises

### Promise 1: We Will Always Choose Compassion

```
When:
  System says: "AUTO_REJECT (ambiguous case)"
  Ethics say: "HUMAN_REVIEW (give benefit of doubt)"

Decision: HUMAN_REVIEW (always)
```

**Because:** The cost of false rejection is harm to an innocent person.  
The cost of false approval is... a second look.

### Promise 2: We Will Share Abundance

```
When:
  Market says: "Create scarcity to increase value"
  Ethics say: "Scale generously to help more"

Decision: SCALE GENEROUSLY (always)
```

**Because:** Hoarding opportunities is theft from the desperate.

### Promise 3: We Will Keep Humans in the Loop

```
When:
  AI says: "I can decide alone"
  Ethics say: "Humans should review hard calls"

Decision: HUMAN REVIEW (always)
```

**Because:** Algorithms don't understand suffering. People do.

### Promise 4: We Will Be Radically Transparent

```
When:
  Business says: "Hide the algorithm"
  Ethics say: "Explain every decision"

Decision: EXPLAIN ALWAYS (always)
```

**Because:** Transparency is accountability. Secrecy is corruption.

### Promise 5: We Will Help People Escape, Not Stay

```
When:
  Economy says: "Keep them dependent on your platform"
  Ethics say: "Help them graduate and move on"

Decision: HELP THEM GRADUATE (always)
```

**Because:** Success means: "I don't need OneHeart anymore. I have real work, real income, real dignity."

## The Three Sacred Refusals

### 1. We Refuse to Be Evil

> **Refusal:** We will never deliberately harm vulnerable people for profit.

**Operationally:**
- No harsh penalties for poor people
- No "gotcha" rules designed to trap players
- No predatory pricing or paywalls for essentials
- No manipulation disguised as "engagement"

### 2. We Refuse to Be Selfish

> **Refusal:** We will never hoard resources that could help more people.

**Operationally:**
- No artificial limits on rewards
- No scarcity-driven pricing
- No exclusive access tiers for rich
- No gatekeeping of knowledge or skills

### 3. We Refuse to Be Cowardly

> **Refusal:** We will never hide behind technology to avoid responsibility.

**Operationally:**
- Monthly public ethics audits
- Fast response to complaints
- Genuine apologies when wrong
- Commitment to fix, not defend

## The Origin of OneHeart

**Why does OneHeart exist?**

Because there are people who:
- Are bored with their lives
- Want meaningful work but can't find it
- Have skills but no way to use them
- Feel left behind by the economy
- Long for community and purpose
- Deserve dignity and respect

**OneHeart exists to say:** *"You matter. Your skills matter. Your community needs you. Let's find what's next together."*

**Not:** *"Give us your data so we can extract value."*  
**Not:** *"Compete with thousands for scraps."*  
**Not:** *"Accept whatever work is available."*

**But:** *"Let's listen to your hopes. Let's find work that matches your values. Let's build something real together."*
